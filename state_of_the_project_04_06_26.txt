current best claim: a physics-informed residual predictor that works well on simulated held-out data, can ingest real telemetry, and can be adapted to off-nominal propulsion conditions by updating the physics/simulation domain. The method is viable, and performance improves when the physics/training domain matches the flown configuration.


reasoning: Because the real flight occurred under off-nominal propulsion conditions not represented in the original training set, a matched synthetic domain was generated from the measured/estimated motor configuration. The adapted evaluation demonstrates that the hybrid GRU-RK4 architecture is sensitive to physically meaningful propulsion parameters and benefits from corrected physics priors. This supports the feasibility of the approach, while broader generalization across propulsion states remains future work.

strongest claims: 
1. Synthetic 3D validation is strong.

Updating the RK4/motor prior using the actual .rse motor configuration improved real-flight replay performance.
